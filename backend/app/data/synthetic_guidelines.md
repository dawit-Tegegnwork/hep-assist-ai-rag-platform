# Synthetic HEP Guideline Snippets

These snippets are fabricated for a portfolio backend demo. They are not clinical guidance.

## Hepatitis B Screening

Screen synthetic adult profiles with liver risk factors using HBsAg, anti-HBs, and anti-HBc. Confirm positives with follow-up testing. Document screening date and result in the community health worker log.

## Hepatitis C Testing

Offer one-time HCV antibody screening in adult synthetic scenarios, then reflex to RNA confirmation before documenting active infection. If antibody is reactive, schedule RNA test within two weeks in the demo workflow.

## Liver Safety Monitoring

Escalate synthetic cases with jaundice, abdominal pain, confusion, or severe lab abnormalities to a supervising clinician immediately. Do not attempt diagnosis in the field.

## Community Health Worker Triage

Use the approved triage checklist for low-connectivity settings: assess danger signs first, record vitals if available, refer urgent cases, and schedule follow-up for stable patients within 72 hours.

## Vaccination Education

Provide education on hepatitis B vaccination using approved pamphlets only. Document that education was given; do not recommend off-label dosing or contraindications not in the approved content.

## Medication Safety

Review synthetic medication lists for hepatotoxicity risk flags. Flag potential interactions for clinician review. Never change doses without approved protocol reference.

## Referral Pathways

Refer patients with abnormal liver panels or positive hepatitis screens to the nearest facility with lab confirmation capacity. Include reason for referral and date of last contact.

## Low-Connectivity Workflow

When network is unavailable, queue questions locally and sync when connectivity returns. Use approved offline content cache for common screening and education topics.

## Pregnancy and Hepatitis Screening

In synthetic prenatal scenarios, document hepatitis B surface antigen status at first visit. Follow approved referral pathway for positive results; do not provide treatment advice in the field.

## Pediatric Vaccination Schedule

Follow the approved synthetic immunization schedule for hepatitis B birth dose and catch-up vaccination. Document lot number and administration site in the demo log.

## Infection Prevention

Use standard precautions for all patient encounters. Document needle-stick exposure incidents per approved occupational health protocol and refer for evaluation.

## Follow-Up Scheduling

Schedule follow-up visits for hepatitis screening results within 14 days for reactive screens and 30 days for negative screens in high-risk synthetic profiles.

## Health Worker Safety

Health workers should use approved PPE when indicated. Report exposure incidents through the demo occupational health channel within 24 hours.

## Data Documentation

Record all synthetic patient interactions in the approved log format. Do not store real patient identifiers. Use synthetic IDs only.
